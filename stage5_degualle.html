📜 HISTORICAL CONTEXT
Charles de Gaulle Square in Moscow was named in 1966 following the French President's official visit to the Soviet Union. De Gaulle, a staunch anti-communist and WWII hero, pursued an independent foreign policy — in 1966 he withdrew France from NATO's military command, seeking closer ties with the Eastern bloc. The square's naming was a pragmatic diplomatic gesture: the USSR hoped to weaken the Western alliance by encouraging French independence from the United States. This monument reminds us that Cold War politics was not simply "East vs. West" — it also involved strategic courtship of non-aligned or independent-minded leaders.
🇷🇺 Площадь Шарля де Голля в Москве названа в 1966 году после визита президента Франции. Де Голль вывел Францию из военного командования НАТО, и СССР использовал это для ослабления Западного альянса. Площадь — символ прагматичной холодной войны.
