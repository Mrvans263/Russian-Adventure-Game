📜 HISTORICAL CONTEXT
Cosmonauts Alley was established in the 1960s near the Monument to the Conquerors of Space. It features bronze busts of Soviet cosmonauts — Yuri Gagarin (first human in space, 1961), Valentina Tereshkova (first woman in space, 1963), and others — along with a statue of Sergei Korolev, the chief rocket engineer who was kept anonymous during his lifetime. The alley served as a state‑crafted hall of heroes, projecting an image of Soviet unity and technological triumph during the Cold War. Behind the celebration, however, the space program involved immense risk, secrecy, and political pressure.
