📍 Rizhsko-Kaluzhskaya Line · Opened 1958
📜 HISTORICAL CONTEXT
VDNKh Metro station opened on May 1, 1958, as part of the Kaluzhsko-Rizhskaya line. Its name refers to the nearby Exhibition of Achievements of the National Economy (VDNKh). The station features stained glass panels with celestial and agricultural motifs — celebrating Soviet progress in space and farming. Unlike Stalin's heavy neoclassical stations (e.g., Komsomolskaya), VDNKh station reflects Khrushchev's "Thaw" with lighter marble and simpler forms. It was designed by architects I.G. Taranov and N.A. Bykova.
